## Recurring blind spots (read these before the next deck)

### 1. GCSE-carryover vocabulary — CONFIRMED (the teacher's own stated weakness)
Across the first run this fired **three times**, on three different topics:
- Slide 5 — "produce energy" for mitochondria, where only ATP is credited (`EI-1`).
- Slide 6 — "controls all the cell's activities" for the nucleus, GCSE-level, zero credit (`EI-7`).
- Slide 9 — "genetic information" for viruses, where AQA requires "genetic material".

This is the weakness the teacher named at intake, now evidenced as a genuine recurrence rather
than a one-off. **Next run: scan every function/definition caption against the credit-vocabulary
list and the never-credit tier *first*** (`../editor/reference/spec/3.2-cell-structure.md`
credit-terms tiers). It is this teacher's most reliable source of avoidable mark loss.

### 2. Misconceptions the teacher does not see — WIDER THAN THE SELF-DIAGNOSIS
The teacher's self-diagnosis was vocabulary only. The read found conceptual errors they had **not**
flagged:
- Slide 10 (CRITICAL) — magnification given as the cause of the optical microscope's limit; the
  real cause is resolution (`EI-5`). A reversed causal model, not a wording slip.
- Slide 9 — acellular and non-living fused and justified with one reason (`EI-3`).

**Next run: do not assume the teacher's stated worry is the whole story — check causal-explanation
and definition slides for reversed or fused concepts, not just terminology.** This teacher reads
their own biology as correct (it usually is) and therefore misses where a *correct-sounding* slide
still reinforces the examiner-flagged error.

### 3. Assessed formats sent to practice unrehearsed
Content is taught correctly but the *form the exam takes* is not built in:
- Slide 8 — a features list with no all-vs-some flag, which the list-rule question zeroes (`EI-2`, exam Q3).
- Slide 11 — the magnification equation sent straight to independent questions with no worked
  model, so the separate unit-conversion mark is never rehearsed (`Rosenshine 4`, exam Q7).

**Next run: check lists against the all-vs-some rule, and check every calculation/procedure for a
worked model before independent practice.**

### 4. Lesson-structure habits
- **Retrieval placement** (slide 2) — opens on new content (copying) rather than retrieving prior
  learning. **Next run: read the starter for retrieval-vs-presentation before the content slides.**
- **Coverage sequencing** (slide 3, PROVISIONAL) — cell fractionation absent from a first lesson;
  may be deliberate. Held as an open question, not a confirmed fault. **Next run: confirm where
  fractionation lands in this teacher's unit sequence.**
