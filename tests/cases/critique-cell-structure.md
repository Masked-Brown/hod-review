# Read-through — Cell structure (fixture)

Intake: a Year 12 introduction to 3.2 Cell structure; goal — organelle ultrastructure
and the electron microscope; 50 minutes; the teacher flagged that calculations worry
them. This is a gate-test fixture, not a constructed run.

# Findings

SEVERITY: CRITICAL
SLIDE: 4
QUOTE: "To see smaller organelles, increase the magnification."
PRINCIPLE: —
SPEC: AQA 3.2.1 — EI-5
WHY: This is the resolution/magnification confusion AQA reports year on year (EI-5): the slide presents increasing magnification as the way to see smaller organelles, so a class copying it revises the wrong cause and drops the mark that turns on resolution.
QUESTION: Where in the lesson does the class meet resolution as the real reason the light microscope cannot separate smaller organelles, so this slide is challenged rather than reinforced?

SEVERITY: MAJOR
SLIDE: 3
QUOTE: "Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell and produce energy for respiration."
PRINCIPLE: —
SPEC: AQA 3.2.1 — ATP not "energy"
WHY: AQA credits ATP and rejects "produce energy" outright (EI-1); this is the caption the class copies into their notes, so the rejected word is the one they will revise from.
QUESTION: How will the class leave writing "ATP" here rather than "energy", given this is the wording on the slide they keep?

SEVERITY: MAJOR
SLIDE: 5
QUOTE: "Now try the questions on the worksheet."
PRINCIPLE: Rosenshine 4 — Provide models
SPEC: AQA 3.2.1 — exam Q7
WHY: The equation goes straight to independent practice with no worked example first, and AQA pays the mm→µm unit conversion as its own mark (exam Q7); a class that has never watched the conversion modelled tends to drop it under time pressure.
QUESTION: What worked calculation does the class see before this worksheet, and does it rehearse the separate unit-conversion step the mark scheme credits?
